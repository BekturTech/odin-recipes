Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak ('five finger') is a dish from Central Asian cuisine. It is also known as naryn in Xinjiang, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan, as turama or dograma in Karakalpakstan, North Caucasus and Turkmenistan, as kullama in Bashkortostan and Tatarstan. It is a one of main national dish of Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan.

Borsok

In the Central Asian country of Kyrgyzstan, honoring the dead is an important part of the culture, and one way to connect with the afterlife is through a fried bread called borsok.
